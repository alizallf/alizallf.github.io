Overview

Project Harmony was initiated to address declining user engagement and satisfaction with the client's wellness platform. The existing interface was cluttered, navigation was confusing, and the overall experience felt outdated.

My role as Lead UX Designer involved conducting user research, creating wireframes and prototypes, collaborating with developers, and testing the new design with users.

The Challenge

  • 1

    User engagement had dropped by 32% over the previous six months

  • 2

    Customer satisfaction scores were at an all-time low of 6.2/10

  • 3

    The platform needed to accommodate new features without adding complexity

Research

I conducted a comprehensive research phase including:

User Interviews

Conducted 15 in-depth interviews with current users to understand pain points

Competitive Analysis

Analyzed 5 competitor platforms to identify industry best practices

Usability Testing

Ran 8 usability tests on the existing platform to identify specific issues

Survey

Collected 250+ responses from users about their needs and preferences

Key Insights

  • Users found the navigation confusing and inconsistent

  • Content organization didn't match users' mental models

  • Visual hierarchy was poor, making it difficult to scan content

Design Process

Wireframing

Created low-fidelity wireframes to explore different navigation patterns and content organization. Tested these with users to validate our approach before moving to high-fidelity designs.

Visual Design

Developed a clean, modern visual language with improved typography, spacing, and color usage. Created a component library to ensure consistency across the platform.

Prototyping & Testing

Created interactive prototypes and conducted usability testing with 12 participants. Iterated on the design based on feedback to address usability issues.

Interactive Prototype

The redesigned platform features a clean, intuitive interface with improved navigation, clear visual hierarchy, and a modern aesthetic that aligns with the brand's values.

Learnings

This project provided valuable insights and learnings that I'll carry forward to future work:

1

Early user involvement is crucial

Involving users from the beginning of the process helped us identify issues we wouldn't have discovered otherwise.

2

Iterative testing pays off

Multiple rounds of testing and refinement led to a much stronger final product than if we had tested only once.

3

Cross-functional collaboration

Working closely with developers and stakeholders throughout the process ensured a smoother implementation.
